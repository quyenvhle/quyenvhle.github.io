Transforming ARI Fleet Management


From September 2018 to December 2018, I worked as a Business Analyst & Design Lead in a four-month project with four other students to help Automotive Rental Inc. (ARI) establish a new digital portal called ARI Sprint.

Background

ARI is the largest privately held fleet management company in the world focused on providing sustainable fleet practices, with the mission to deliver excellent customer service and employ transparent data-sharing. The companyâ€™s primary clients are organizations that require a large, well-managed fleet of vehicles for core business functions, like FedEx and cable companies. ARI was looking to diversify its product portfolio with a new program, through which non-traditional clients like financial institutions and credit unions can have their vehicles remarket easily. Therefore, our team was on the quest to design a client-facing portal that could help facilitate this remarketing process between ARI and these new market segment.

Understanding Needs

During this exploratory phase, our team had a kick-off meeting with six stakeholders from ARI's Marketing, Supply Chain & Operations, and Technology teams, as well as two 90-minute follow-ups. According to the stakeholders, they wanted a portal that could partially help offset the manual work inherited in the process and increase positive customer experience. Traditionally, clients had to get on the phone with customer service agents when they wanted to be on the client list, to add a new vehicle to their resell portfolio, to know the statuses of their vehicles, and to ask questions. As a result, a remarketing cycle from start to finish often lasted around 34 days. ARI looked to decrease this number to 28 days within a year of implementing the new portal. Therefore, the portal needs to be intuitive enough for clients to self-manage theirs without much assistance from ARI side.

Personas

Brainstorming & Prioritizing

What came to my mind initially was a dashboard that clients can immediately see when they log in. Having this dashboard as a centralized space for vehicle management would save clients a lot of time: instead of browsing through this and that, they could get the needed information in just a glimpse. With this dashboard, clients should also be able to schedule pickups, view statuses of remarketed vehicles, and manage their vehicle portfolios.

In addition, the back-and-forth communication between clients and agents is one reason why the remarketing process takes that long. Therefore, having a chatbot which answers clients' questions could be valuable to ARI.

What's Next?

We presented the prototype to the ARI team, and the team was pretty pleased with our proposal. They also suggested adding an automated onboarding walkthrough to the list of features since ARI Sprint was a brand new portal. We incorporated the suggestion and handed the final deliverable to the in-house team who took over the implementation phase.

Reflection

Design is something that should not be intuitive but must be approached systematically. A good design takes time and serves as a crucial part of product development. I felt especially thankful for Donald Norman's "The Design of Everyday Things" for laying foundation of good designs for me.

There's also a huge difference between designing something entirely new and improving upon something. With this new portal, besides its functionality and usability, I also need to design its data model, which presents what data is required and what format is to be used, and business rules, which specifies the constraints aroung ARI Sprint. it are, and then document them properly. Doing so allows ARI to better integrate the new data with their existing corporate database and at the same time, govern how different information is presented to different types of customers.
