Winning The Back-to-college Season


The Target Case Competition is sponsored by Target Corporation & Center for Student Professional Development. This year’s prompt centers around helping Target increase its competitive advantage in the Back-to-College season. Over the course of one month, I collaborated with three other students to develop a 10-page executive report detailing our recommendations. Our submission got selected as one of the 5 finalists to present in front of a panel of Target judges, and won 1st place out of 15 teams. The full slides deck can be found here.

Background

With $25.5 billion in planned spending in 2018, the Back-to-College season is considered one of the biggest shopping season of the year. Key players in the industry, such as Walmart, IKEA, Amazon, are using innovative ways to drive sales, foot traffic and great guest experiences. The increase in competition from both brick-and-mortar and online retailers poses a question to Target: How can the company differentiate itself and achieve results throughout stores that vary in physical size, volume, location and complexity?

Research

As a retailer, Target does a great job with engaging college students through shopping parties and college stylists on Youtube. However, compared to its competitors, Target's e-commerce capabilities is underutilized, and there exists an untapped opportunity to engage with parents, the ones with high purchasing power.

Solution

In order to address these issues, we decided that the goal should be to nurture the parent & student relationship, which would give Target a competitive advantage to capture more of the market share, increase guest retention and convert shoppers to lifetime loyal guests. With that in mind, we came up with CollegeUp, a three-stage plan that blends together emotional storytelling and digital innovations.

Raise Awareness

With 50% of BTC shopping took place before the school year starts, it is important for Target to capture guests' attention early. In “Raising Awareness", Target can build and maintain a strong online and offline presence by investing in paid partnership and promotional contents. For the first part, paid partnership, we suggest Target to partner with micro-influencers whose main contents focus on lifestyle, beauty, decorations, DIYs.

Target should also focus on running ads through online and offline channels, traditional and modern channels to drive higher traffic to its website and physical stores. In addition, based on the data collected from these platforms, Target can personalize ads and present more relevant recommendations to prospective guests, encouraging more purchases to be made.

Encourage Participation

With guests knowing about the campaign, Target can convert them into participants through phase 2. Since part of Target’s core guests are millennials with interests in technology trends, we propose the implementation of Virtual Rooms at 3 biggest Target stores. Through using a VR headset, guest can design their dream rooms with Target’s catalog of household items, furniture, and decorations and actually see how the designs will turn out.

In addition, Target can partner with local university to hold workshops during move in week. As going into college is a big milestone, participating in the workshops promise to bring students and parent good memories and quality time together before school begins. Moreover, bringing products to these workshops and letting participants try them out will also help Target drive sales of these featured items.

Sustain Engagement

While the Back-to-college hype only lasts from July to September, the bond between parents and their children extends beyond the season. Therefore, we suggest that Target expand the existing subscription model to include monthly care packages - parcels of products that parents can customize and send to their children. With 60% of parents wish to spread out their budget, care packages serve as an efficient way for these guests to allocate spending throughout the year, while nurturing the relationships with their children. The new offering also allows Target to promote the use of REDCards, which give discounts on subscription-based items, and thus, pose an opportunity to convert these parents into lifelong guests.


What's Next?

Our team had a talk with Target marketing managers after the award ceremony and they were impressed with the whole plan, especially the care packages idea. It was still unclear if they decided to move forward with the implementation phase; however, I'm still grateful for the opportunity to help the retailer develop a marketing plan from the perspective of a student, Back-to-College season's main audience.
