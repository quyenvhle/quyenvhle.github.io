Showcasing Choreographies Through Hyphen


When programmers want to showcase their code, they upload it on GitHub. When designers want to exhibit their designs, they do that on Behance or Dribbble. However, when dancers want to display their dance footage, they do not have a specialized space for that purpose. Through Hyphen, I hope to solve that problem by creating a way for dancers to showcase and discover choreographies.

Target Users

The ideal users are dancers and dance enthusiasts whose ages vary from 15 to 40. They have active lifestyles, value originality and uniqueness, love to learn, live in cities with big art communities, and are members of those communities. Although they come from diverse educational and income backgrounds, they share a passion and appreciation for dance movements. These users like to choreograph dance pieces, join/teach dance classes, share their works online, and follow other dancers. They want to share their choreographies and browse dance footage for inspiration, but currently lack a way to easily do so.
